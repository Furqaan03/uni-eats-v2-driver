# Product

## Register

product

## Users

University student delivery drivers at UDST Qatar. They glance at the screen while managing deliveries — switching between map focus and status checks. They use the app standing outside restaurants, on motorbikes between stops, or waiting in a parking lot. Ambient conditions vary: bright outdoor sun, dim indoor light, moving fast. Every extra tap is friction. Every unclear status is stress.

## Product Purpose

A driver utility that lets students earn money doing campus deliveries. Core jobs: go online, accept orders, track delivery steps, monitor earnings. Success = driver completes a shift without the app slowing them down or confusing them.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, fast, trustworthy. The app should feel like a pro tool, not a student project. Confident typography, clear hierarchy, no decorative noise. When something needs attention it earns attention — everything else stays quiet.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard aesthetics: hero metric cards, gradient text, stacked info-box grids
- Consumer food-app warmth: Talabat/Zomato reds, oranges, food photography, bubbly corners
- Map-app clutter: overlapping layers, too many controls visible at once
- Glassmorphism as decoration
- Identical card grids

## Design Principles

1. **One truth at a time.** The screen surface that's most active right now owns all the attention. Map when idle, order card when a request arrives, step tracker during delivery.
2. **Status above all.** Online/Offline and active delivery state are never ambiguous. Color and position reinforce them without text alone carrying the load.
3. **Earn the space.** Every element visible on the home screen must justify its pixel cost. Earnings in idle mode are ambient data, not the hero.
4. **Controlled color.** A single saturated accent (green for live/active) against near-black. Color carries meaning, not decoration.
5. **Fast at a glance.** Numbers and labels at sufficient scale to read in 0.5 seconds. No squinting, no zooming.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. High contrast between status indicators and dark backgrounds. Tap targets minimum 44pt. Arabic locale consideration for right-to-left layout where needed (future).
